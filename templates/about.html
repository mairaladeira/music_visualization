Explanation of the project: This project aims to create different visualizations for music data. The data is collected from the authors Last.fm account and worked with the help of Python 3 and JavaScript in order to created representative visualizations. The focus of this project is the genres of the songs that are represented by the following colors:
  • Jazz/blues
  • Pop
  • Metal
  • Rock
  • Hip-hop/Rap
  • Dance/Electronic
  • Alternative/Indie
  • R&B/Soul
  • Other

The Comparison page contains visualizations that compare the data from the 3 users. Each of the developed visualization is described bellow:


Main Visualization (Stacked Tile Maps): Stacked Tile Maps is a variation of Tile Maps, in this graph we attempted to represent four dimensions: days, hours, genres and frequency of songs. x-axis represents days,y-axis represents hours of the day in 24 hour format, the color gives the genre of the listened songs, and the amount of songs listened per hour and day are represented as a stack in each tile.


Stacked daily bar histogram: We introduced a stacked bar histogram on a daily basis, its x-axis represents the days while it y-axis represents the frequency of songs per day. This histogram not only shows the amount of songs listened per day, it also depicts genreâ€™s changes over time by keeping the color of the listened songs accordingly to their genres. An innovative feature added to this common data representation is that by clicking one or more of the links located above the chart, a transparent black window is displayed where we can discover the genres progress over time and compare the frequency of listened songs for particular genres with either the total amount of songs provided in the background with the histogram or between them.


Stacked hourly bar histogram: A stacked hourly histogram of genres reveals different insights about the data, such as the preferred genres in each hour and the variation of genres listened per hour. The x-axis represents the hours in a 24 hour format and the y-axis shows the frequency of each listened genre. Additionally, a mouse-hover functionality displays the percentage of the listened genre on the hour.

Genres bars histogram: It is a simple bar histogram representing a general overview of the frequency of listened genres in the overall time. On x-axis it represents genres and on y-axis represents the amount of songs played by a user for each genre. The bars in the histogram are colored according to the color of genre.

Word clouds: An artists word cloud, with the purpose of highlight the artists listened the most by each of the authors of this work. The size of the word illustrating the artist name is proportional to the number of times the user has listened to that artist, whereas the color of the word depicts the artists genre.


Venn diagrams: Two venn diagrams are proposed, one intersecting the common number of artists between the users and the other the common number of songs. Hover, over the intersections gives the number of artists or songs in common whereas over the name of the user gives the total amount of artists or songs the user has.


Genres timelines: Composed by simple line graphs representing and comparing the evolution of the genres of each author over the time. In this chart, x-axis represents hours of the day in a 24 hours format and y-axis represents the frequency of listening to a specific genre. This interactive plot gives the opportunity to compare different genres between users, by choosing the genre above the chart. Green line represents Gabriela, pink and purple represent Maira and Mehreen respectively.
